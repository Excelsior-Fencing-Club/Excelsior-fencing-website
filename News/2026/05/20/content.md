### Program Overview

The Ontario Fencing Association (OFA) is delivering the first stages of the coaching pathway through the **Community Instructor** and **Instructor Beginner** training and certification program.  
  
These courses are aligned with the NCCP framework and are designed to provide foundational coaching skills, progressing from group instruction to introductory individual lesson delivery.

- **Community Instructor (Level 1):** Entry-level certification focused on safety, basic fencing instruction, and group lesson delivery. _One weekend (June 13–14)._
- **Instructor Beginner (Level 2):** Builds on foundational skills with an introduction to individual lessons, technical progression, and athlete development. _Two weekends (June 13–14 and June 28–29)

### Program Structure

**Weekend 1 – Foundations (12 hours)**  
**Focus:** Safety, group management, warm-up design, footwork, and basic technical instruction.  
  
**Participants will:**

- Learn how to structure and deliver safe, engaging group sessions
- Develop fundamental coaching skills (demonstration, observation, correction)
- Build and deliver complete beginner-level lessons
- Be introduced to technical progressions and coaching communication

**Evaluation (Community Instructor):**

- Delivery of warm-up, footwork, and group lesson components
- Demonstration of safe and effective coaching practices
- Completion of workbook/portfolio

  
**Weekend 2 – Instructor Beginner (12 hours)**  
**Focus:** Skill progression, individual lessons, and performance-oriented coaching.  
  
**Participants will:**

- Develop structured individual lessons (introduction, development, feedback)
- Improve technical analysis and correction skills
- Apply progression in footwork, tactics, and blade work
- Gain experience through supervised coaching rotations

**Evaluation (Instructor Beginner):**

- Delivery of a complete individual lesson
- Assessment based on progression, clarity, corrections, and communication
- Participation in video analysis and performance review|

### Key Learning Outcomes

By the end of the program, coaches will be able to:

- Plan and deliver safe, structured fencing sessions
- Teach fundamental fencing skills using clear progressions
- Manage groups effectively in a club environment
- Deliver introductory individual lessons
- Provide appropriate feedback and technical corrections

### Approach & Methodology

The program emphasizes experiential learning, combining:

- Demonstrations by Learning Facilitators
- Small-group collaboration and lesson planning
- Practical coaching delivery with peer feedback
- Guided reflection and continuous improvement

### Next Steps in the Coaching Pathway

Upon successful certification, coaches will be prepared to:

- Coach beginner and developing athletes in club environments
- Continue toward higher NCCP certification levels
- Engage in ongoing mentorship and professional development opportunities
